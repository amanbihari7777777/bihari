Districts of Bihar - Tourism & Specialties

Introduction

Bihar has 38 districts, each with its own cultural, historical, and economic significance. Here's a look at what makes each district special along with their major tourist attractions.

1. Patna

Specialty: Capital city, administrative and educational hub.

Tourism: Golghar, Patna Sahib Gurudwara, Kumhrar, Agam Kuan, Patna Museum.

2. Gaya

Specialty: Religious significance in Hinduism and Buddhism.

Tourism: Mahabodhi Temple, Vishnupad Temple, Dungeshwari Cave Temples.

3. Nalanda

Specialty: Home to the ancient Nalanda University.

Tourism: Nalanda University ruins, Hiuen Tsang Memorial Hall, Surya Mandir.

4. Vaishali

Specialty: Birthplace of Lord Mahavira.

Tourism: Ashokan Pillar, Buddha Stupa, Raja Vishal Ka Garh.

5. Rajgir

Specialty: Ancient city linked to Buddha and Mahavira.

Tourism: Vishwa Shanti Stupa, Hot Springs, Gridhakuta Hill.

6. Bhagalpur

Specialty: Silk industry hub, known for Tussar silk.

Tourism: Vikramshila University, Mandar Hill, Ajgaivinath Temple.

7. Muzaffarpur

Specialty: Famous for its Shahi Litchis.

Tourism: Garibnath Temple, Jubba Sahni Park, Baba Garibnath Mandir.

8. Purnia

Specialty: Major agricultural hub, especially maize production.

Tourism: Jalalgarh Fort, Kali Mandir, Kosi River area.

9. Madhubani

Specialty: World-famous Madhubani paintings.

Tourism: Kapileshwar Temple, Uchaitha Kali Temple.

10. Sitamarhi

Specialty: Believed to be the birthplace of Goddess Sita.

Tourism: Janaki Temple, Haleshwar Sthan, Pupri.

Conclusion

Bihar is a state with a deep-rooted history and diverse tourism attractions. Each district has something unique to offer, making it a great place to explore.
